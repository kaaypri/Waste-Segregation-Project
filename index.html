How to Manage Waste

1

Reduce Waste Generation

Minimize before it starts

• Avoid single-use plastics — carry reusable bags.

• Buy only what you need to prevent food waste.

• Choose products with recyclable packaging.

• Use digital receipts instead of paper.

2

Segregate at Source

Separate wet, dry & hazardous

• Keep separate bins: Green (wet), Blue (dry), Red (hazardous).

• Rinse containers before dry waste.

• Never mix medical waste with regular waste.

• Use EcoSort's smart detection to verify.

3

Reuse & Repurpose

Give items a second life

• Convert jars into storage containers.

• Donate usable clothes, books & electronics.

• Use newspaper for packing.

• Repurpose t-shirts as cleaning rags.

4

Compost Organic Waste

Turn waste into fertilizer

• Set up a compost bin in your backyard.

• Add fruit peels, veggie scraps, leaves.

• Avoid composting meat or dairy.

• Use compost as natural fertilizer.

5

Recycle Properly

Know what's recyclable

• Check recycling symbols on packaging.

• Flatten cardboard boxes to save space.

• Remove caps from bottles before recycling.

• Sell recyclables to local scrap dealer.

6

Dispose Responsibly

Final step matters most

• Hand segregated waste to municipal collectors.

• Drop hazardous waste at collection centers.

• Never burn waste — releases toxic gases.

• Report illegal dumping to authorities.

Segregation Guide

What is Waste Segregation?

Separating waste into wet, dry, and hazardous categories at source. Ensures efficient recycling and reduces landfill load.

Why Segregate?

Prevents contamination of recyclables.
Reduces landfill waste by up to 60%.
Enables proper hazardous treatment.
Saves energy & natural resources.
Creates recycling sector jobs.

Reduces disease spread. Minimizes breeding grounds for mosquitoes & rodents.

Reduces overflowing bins, clogged drains, urban flooding, and improves quality of life.
